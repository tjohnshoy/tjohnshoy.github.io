Travel on Your Terms.

User Research

We began our research by interviewing six individuals that were selected based on the established proto persona. The interview questions were aimed at learning the steps people take while trip planning, their priorities, and ultimately, which parts of the process were creating pain points or obstacles. In addition to the interviews, we collected survey data from 30 respondents to broaden our understanding our user.

Our research gave us a clear picture of the target audience for this application. The key demographics were adults ages 25-45, working full time, who travel 1-3 times per year. The user persona is a young professional who is all about having unique and authentic experiences while traveling. She is frustrated by hours spent researching activities, attractions and restaurants.

Definition

Travel apps and websites have been designed to help travelers plan the details of their vacations; However, we learned that the modern traveler finds the planning process to be cumbersome, and values spontaneous experiences. The key pain points identified were the time and energy needed for planning, sifting through reviews and finding unique or authentic experiences.

How might we reduce the pre-planning workload and empower travelers to be spontaneous while they are on-the-go in their travel destination?

Ideation

We brainstormed potential features and tools that could address the goals and paint points for the user. The ideas were categorized into a value matrix based on how closely they aligned with the research we gathered. We also referenced our competitor analysis to see the strengths and weaknesses of other apps in the marketplace, and where we could best offer an improved user experience.

After compiling and reviewing all of the data, three main features were determined to best fit the needs of the user: An itinerary builder, an itinerary library, and a review system which allows for user endorsement.

Prototyping

Prior to creating our low and mid-fidelity prototypes, we created a user flow diagram, user scenarios and user journey maps to plot out the necessary pages for the app, as well as the steps a user might take in a real world situation. We returned to our user persona to empathize with the feelings of our traveler and imagine the various scenarios where we could apply our solution to the users problem.


User Testing

The objective for my guerilla testing was to determine if users can successfully create a trip, find the favorites section, add an itinerary to their trip, and apply filters to their search. I tested these tasks as they are the primary function of the app, and the usability of these features is key to ensuring users will repeatedly use the app for travel.

Final Thoughts

When we began researching for this project, I was surprised to find that the average user values and enjoys some spontaneity while they are traveling. Based on the research, I believe people will continue to migrate towards a more spontaneous mode of travelling since smartphones put the power in the users hands to make decisions in the moment.

Although this app addresses a user need, there is a great deal of opportunity to expand the offerings in the future. A few such opportunities include integration with ride sharing apps like Lyft and Uber, purchasing capabilities, and personality profiles to enable user suggestions.
